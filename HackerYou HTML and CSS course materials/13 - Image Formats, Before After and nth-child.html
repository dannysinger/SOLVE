Working with images

When creating sites, we work with many different types of images. Choosing the right format of image is based on the needs of the design.

JPG/JPEG

The most common image format within websites, JPG’s are used for a large majority of images that appear as background images, inline images and more. A lossy format, JPG’s can be optimized to small sizes and cannot include transparencies.

Fun fact JPEG stands for Joint Photographic Experts Group. What?

GIF

Graphic Interchange Format, commonly known in it’s animated form, GIF’s can be used to simulate transparencies, however the cleanliness of GIF’s is limited and may lead to designs with pixelated images with a larger file size.

PNG

Portable Network Graphics, or PNG is a relatively new addition to the field of development. Best utilized for images that require transparencies, PNG’s have a smaller image size than GIF’s and have browser support back to IE7.

::before and ::after elements

We have already learned that we have pseudo selectors: :hover, :focus etc..

We also have ::before and ::after - they are what we call pesudo elements`. These are added in CSS and create elements before or after that can be used to add stylistic/cosmetic touches.

These elements take a content property:

<span class="name">Drew Minns</span>

<style>
	span.name::before {
		content : '☃';	
	}
</style>
